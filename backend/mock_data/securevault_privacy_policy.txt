SECUREVAULT LTD. — PRIVACY AND DATA PROTECTION POLICY
Effective Date: 1 June 2024 | Version 2.3

1. INTRODUCTION

SecureVault Ltd. ("SecureVault," "we," "us") is a UK-based provider of document
management and digital vault services. This policy describes how we handle personal
data that we process on behalf of our business customers in connection with our
SaaS platform.

2. DATA CONTROLLER / PROCESSOR RELATIONSHIP

When providing our platform to enterprise customers, SecureVault acts as a data
processor and our customers act as the data controllers. We process personal data
solely in accordance with customer instructions for the purposes of delivering the
contracted service.

We enter into a data processing agreement with enterprise customers upon request.
Customers should contact their account manager to arrange execution of a DPA.
Our standard service agreement does not incorporate GDPR Article 28 compliant DPA
language by default; a separate addendum must be requested and negotiated.

3. DATA WE PROCESS

On behalf of customers, we may process personal data contained within documents
uploaded to our platform, including names, identification numbers, financial data,
and other business records. The categories of data processed depend entirely on
the content of documents stored by customers.

4. INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS

SecureVault's primary infrastructure is hosted on AWS within the UK and EU (eu-west-1,
eu-central-1). Where data is processed outside the UK or EEA, we rely on standard
contractual clauses or other appropriate transfer mechanisms as required by applicable
law. Customers with specific data residency requirements should notify us at the
time of contracting to ensure appropriate infrastructure configuration.

5. DATA RETENTION

SecureVault retains customer data for the duration of the contract. Following contract
termination or expiry, customer data is deleted from our primary systems within a
reasonable period. Customers may request deletion of specific documents at any time
via the platform. Backup copies of data are retained for a period consistent with
our operational backup schedule; the specific retention period for backup data varies
and customers should contact us for further details.

6. SUB-PROCESSORS

SecureVault uses third-party service providers to support the delivery of our
platform. Our current sub-processors include infrastructure and hosting providers,
email delivery services, and operational monitoring tools. A list of our key
sub-processors is available upon written request to privacy@securevault.io. We
reserve the right to engage new sub-processors in the ordinary course of business;
customers who wish to be notified of changes to our sub-processor list should
subscribe to our notification service.

7. SECURITY MEASURES

We implement technical and organisational measures appropriate to the nature of
the data processed on our platform, including encryption at rest and in transit,
access controls, and incident response procedures. Further detail on our security
measures is available in our Vendor Security Questionnaire, which can be requested
from our sales team.

8. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

Where we receive data subject requests directly from individuals, we will forward
these to the relevant customer controller. SecureVault will cooperate with controllers
in fulfilling their obligations to data subjects under applicable data protection law.

9. BREACH NOTIFICATION

In the event of a confirmed personal data breach, SecureVault will notify the
relevant customer without undue delay. Our security team will investigate the incident
and provide affected customers with information necessary for them to fulfil their
regulatory notification obligations.

10. GOVERNING LAW

This policy is governed by the laws of England and Wales.
