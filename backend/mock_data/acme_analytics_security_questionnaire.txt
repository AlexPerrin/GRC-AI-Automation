ACME ANALYTICS INC. — VENDOR SECURITY QUESTIONNAIRE RESPONSE
Prepared for: Procurement / GRC Review Team
Date: January 2025

1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

Acme Analytics Inc. ("Acme") provides enterprise business intelligence and analytics
services delivered as a cloud-hosted SaaS platform. Our infrastructure is hosted on
AWS (ca-central-1 primary, us-east-1 DR) and Google Cloud Platform (europe-west1 for
EU-resident customers). We serve over 400 enterprise customers across North America
and the European Union.

2. SECURITY CERTIFICATIONS AND AUDITS

Acme holds a current SOC 2 Type II report covering the Security, Availability, and
Confidentiality trust service criteria. The most recent audit period ran from
1 October 2023 to 30 September 2024; the report was issued on 15 November 2024 and
is available to prospective customers under NDA upon request.

We engaged an independent CREST-accredited penetration testing firm (Cobalt Security
Ltd.) to perform a full-scope external and internal penetration test in September 2024.
The engagement covered our web application, API layer, and internal network perimeter.
All critical and high findings were remediated prior to go-live; a remediation letter
confirming closure is included in our SOC 2 report package.

3. ACCESS CONTROL

Access to all production systems requires multi-factor authentication (MFA) enforced
via our identity provider (Okta). MFA cannot be bypassed or self-service disabled;
exceptions require written approval from the CISO. We operate a least-privilege
role-based access control (RBAC) model; access reviews are conducted quarterly.
Privileged access to production databases is mediated through a PAM solution
(CyberArk) with full session recording and audit trail.

Customer-facing admin accounts support SSO via SAML 2.0 and OIDC. MFA is mandatory
for all admin roles; end-user MFA is strongly recommended and enabled by default.

4. DATA ENCRYPTION

All data at rest is encrypted using AES-256. Data in transit is protected using
TLS 1.3 (TLS 1.2 minimum; legacy ciphers disabled). Encryption keys are managed
using AWS KMS with automatic annual rotation. Customer-managed key (CMK) options
are available under our Enterprise tier.

5. INCIDENT RESPONSE

Acme maintains a formal Incident Response Plan (IRP), reviewed and tested annually
via tabletop exercises. Our published SLA for customer notification of a confirmed
security incident involving their data is 24 hours from internal confirmation. In
the event of a personal data breach triggering GDPR Article 33 obligations, we
commit to notifying affected Controllers within 72 hours of becoming aware of the
breach, consistent with regulatory requirements.

Our security operations centre (SOC) operates 24/7 with automated alerting via
SIEM (Splunk Enterprise Security). Mean time to detect (MTTD) for critical incidents
is tracked quarterly; current benchmark is under 15 minutes.

6. VULNERABILITY MANAGEMENT

Acme performs continuous automated vulnerability scanning of our production
environment using Qualys. Critical CVEs are patched within 72 hours of vendor
disclosure; high-severity CVEs within 14 days. Dependency scanning (SCA) is
integrated into our CI/CD pipeline using Snyk; builds fail on newly introduced
critical CVEs.

Our application security programme includes mandatory SAST (Semgrep) on every pull
request, DAST scanning against staging environments, and annual threat modelling
workshops per service.

7. BUSINESS CONTINUITY AND DISASTER RECOVERY

Acme's platform is architected for high availability across multiple AWS Availability
Zones. Our RTO target is 1 hour and RPO target is 15 minutes for our primary platform.
Full DR failover is tested bi-annually. Backup integrity is verified via automated
restore tests on a weekly basis. Customer data exports are available on-demand from
the admin console.

8. SUPPLY CHAIN AND THIRD-PARTY RISK

All critical sub-processors are listed in our DPA Annex. We require SOC 2 Type II
or ISO 27001 certification from Tier-1 suppliers and review certifications annually.
We maintain a software composition analysis programme; open-source libraries are
reviewed for licence compliance and known vulnerabilities prior to adoption.
