DATAFLOW INC. — VENDOR SECURITY QUESTIONNAIRE RESPONSE
Prepared for: Procurement / GRC Review Team
Date: December 2024

1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

DataFlow Inc. is a cloud-based data integration and ETL platform provider. Our
platform enables enterprises to connect, transform, and move data across cloud and
on-premises systems. We are headquartered in San Francisco, CA, and serve customers
across North America, Europe, and Asia-Pacific. Our infrastructure is hosted
primarily on AWS (us-west-2 primary, eu-west-1 for EU customers).

2. SECURITY CERTIFICATIONS AND AUDITS

DataFlow Inc. is certified under ISO/IEC 27001:2022 (certificate number:
ISO27K-2024-88312, valid through June 2026). The certification scope covers the
design, development, operation, and support of our cloud data integration platform.

We do not currently hold a SOC 2 report. We are evaluating a SOC 2 Type II audit
engagement with a target completion date in H2 2025.

Regarding independent penetration testing: our most recent third-party penetration
test was conducted in June 2023 by ClearPath Security Ltd. The assessment covered
our web application and API surface. Remediation of findings was completed by
September 2023. We have not commissioned a follow-up penetration test since that
engagement. We plan to conduct our next assessment in 2025, pending budget approval.

3. ACCESS CONTROL

DataFlow enforces role-based access control for all customer environments. Employee
access to production systems requires a hardware token (Yubikey) for privileged
operations. Standard administrative access requires password authentication with
optional MFA; MFA is not currently mandatory for all employee access to production
systems. We are planning a company-wide MFA rollout scheduled for Q2 2025.

Customer admin accounts support SAML 2.0 SSO. End-user MFA is available as an
optional feature. We do not currently enforce MFA for customer accounts by default.

Access to production databases is restricted to our SRE team. Access logs are
retained for 6 months. Quarterly access reviews are conducted for privileged roles.

4. DATA ENCRYPTION

All customer data at rest is encrypted using AES-256. Data in transit is protected
using TLS 1.2 or higher. We do not currently support TLS 1.3 for all endpoints;
a migration is planned for H1 2025. Encryption keys are managed using AWS KMS with
manual rotation on an 18-month cycle.

5. INCIDENT RESPONSE

DataFlow maintains an incident response policy that is reviewed annually. In the
event of a confirmed security incident affecting customer data, our team will notify
affected customers and work with them to assess impact. We do not publish a formal
SLA for customer notification timelines; response times will depend on the scope and
severity of the incident. Customers requiring a contractual notification SLA should
raise this during contract negotiation.

Our engineering team is on-call for critical production incidents. We do not
operate a 24/7 dedicated security operations function; security alerts are triaged
by our on-call engineering rotation outside business hours.

6. VULNERABILITY MANAGEMENT

We perform automated vulnerability scanning using OWASP ZAP integrated into our
CI/CD pipeline. Internal vulnerability assessments are conducted bi-annually using
Tenable.io. Critical CVEs are prioritised for remediation within 30 days of
discovery. We do not have a published critical patch SLA.

Software composition analysis (SCA) is performed using Dependabot for open-source
dependencies. We monitor for known CVEs in our dependency tree but do not currently
block builds on newly introduced vulnerabilities.

7. BUSINESS CONTINUITY AND DISASTER RECOVERY

DataFlow operates across two AWS Availability Zones. Our target RTO is 4 hours and
our RPO is 1 hour. DR failover procedures are documented and tested annually via
tabletop exercise. Automated daily backups are stored in a separate AWS region;
restore testing is performed quarterly.

8. SUPPLY CHAIN AND THIRD-PARTY RISK

We maintain an internal list of critical third-party providers. Key sub-processors
include AWS (infrastructure), Twilio SendGrid (email), and PagerDuty (alerting).
We review supplier certifications at contract renewal. We do not currently have a
formal third-party risk management programme with standardised assessment criteria.
