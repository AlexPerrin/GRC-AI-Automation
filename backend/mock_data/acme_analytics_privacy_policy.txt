ACME ANALYTICS INC. — PRIVACY AND DATA PROCESSING POLICY
Effective Date: 1 March 2025 | Version 4.1

1. INTRODUCTION AND SCOPE

Acme Analytics Inc. ("Acme," "we," "us") provides business intelligence and analytics
services to enterprise customers. This policy describes how we collect, process, store,
and protect personal data on behalf of our customers ("Controllers") and explains the
rights available to individuals ("Data Subjects") whose data we process.

This policy applies to all personal data processed by Acme in connection with the
delivery of our SaaS analytics platform and any professional services engagements.

2. ROLES AND LEGAL BASIS

Acme acts as a Data Processor under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
Article 4(8) and as a Service Provider under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA).
Our customers act as Data Controllers and determine the purposes and means of processing.
We process personal data solely on documented instructions from Controllers.

We enter into a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with each customer that meets the
requirements of GDPR Article 28, including: subject matter, duration, nature and purpose
of processing, type of personal data, categories of Data Subjects, and obligations and
rights of the Controller. Sub-processors are listed in our DPA Annex and updated 30 days
before any material change.

3. DATA WE PROCESS

On behalf of Controllers we may process: names, email addresses, job titles, usage
analytics events, device identifiers, and aggregated behavioural metrics. We do not
process special categories of personal data (Article 9 GDPR) unless specifically
contracted to do so with appropriate safeguards.

4. INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS

Acme is headquartered in Toronto, Canada — recognised by the European Commission as
providing adequate protection under GDPR Article 45 (adequacy decision for Canada /
PIPEDA, commercial organisations). For transfers to our US-based infrastructure
providers, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs, Commission Implementing
Decision 2021/914) incorporated into our sub-processor DPAs. A Transfer Impact
Assessment (TIA) is maintained and available to Controllers on request.

5. SECURITY MEASURES

We implement technical and organisational measures (TOMs) appropriate to the risk,
including:
- AES-256 encryption at rest for all stored personal data
- TLS 1.2+ for data in transit
- Role-based access control (RBAC) with least-privilege enforcement
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) required for all staff accessing production systems
- Annual third-party penetration testing; most recent report (SOC 2 Type II, 2024) available
  under NDA
- Continuous vulnerability scanning and a 30-day critical patch SLA

6. DATA RETENTION AND DELETION

Data is retained for the duration of the customer contract plus 90 days, after which it
is securely deleted using NIST 800-88 media sanitisation guidelines. Customers may
request deletion at any time via our data subject request portal. Deletion is confirmed
in writing within 10 business days.

7. BREACH NOTIFICATION

In the event of a personal data breach, Acme will notify the affected Controller without
undue delay and, where feasible, within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, in
accordance with GDPR Article 33. Notifications include: nature of the breach, categories
and approximate number of Data Subjects affected, likely consequences, and measures
taken or proposed to address the breach.

8. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

Acme supports Controllers in fulfilling Data Subject requests for access, rectification,
erasure, restriction, portability, and objection. Requests received directly by Acme are
forwarded to the relevant Controller within 5 business days.

9. PIPEDA COMPLIANCE (CANADA)

Acme complies with the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act
(PIPEDA). A designated Privacy Officer oversees compliance, handles complaints, and
maintains our records of processing activities (ROPA). Contact: privacy@acmeanalytics.io
