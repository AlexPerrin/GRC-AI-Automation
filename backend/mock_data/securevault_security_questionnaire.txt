SECUREVAULT LTD. — VENDOR SECURITY QUESTIONNAIRE RESPONSE
Prepared for: Procurement / GRC Review Team
Date: February 2025

1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

SecureVault Ltd. is a UK-based document management and digital vault SaaS provider.
We serve enterprise customers in financial services, legal, and professional services
sectors. Our platform is hosted on AWS (eu-west-1 and eu-central-1 regions).

2. ACCESS CONTROL

We enforce role-based access control across all customer environments. User accounts
are provisioned through our admin portal and follow a least-privilege model. Password
policies require a minimum of 12 characters and complexity rules are enforced.

Access to production infrastructure is restricted to the engineering operations team.
All access events are logged and retained for 12 months. We are in the process of
rolling out single sign-on (SSO) integration for enterprise customers. MFA is available
as an optional feature for customer admin accounts.

3. DATA ENCRYPTION

All customer data is encrypted at rest using AES-256. Data in transit is protected
using TLS 1.2 or higher. Encryption keys are managed using AWS Key Management Service
(KMS). Key rotation is performed on an annual basis.

4. INCIDENT RESPONSE

SecureVault maintains an incident response plan reviewed by the security team. In the
event of a confirmed security incident affecting customer data, we will notify affected
customers as soon as reasonably practicable. Our security team investigates all reported
incidents and applies remediation measures to prevent recurrence.

We classify incidents by severity internally. Critical incidents are escalated to
senior management. Post-incident reviews are conducted for major events. We do not
currently publish a formal incident response SLA for customer notification timelines.

5. VULNERABILITY MANAGEMENT

We conduct internal vulnerability assessments on a quarterly basis using automated
scanning tools. Critical and high-severity vulnerabilities are prioritised for
remediation. Our patching policy targets critical patches within 30 days of vendor
disclosure.

We have not commissioned an independent third-party penetration test in the past 24
months. We are evaluating external security assessment providers and plan to conduct
a penetration test within the next fiscal year. Our application security practices
include code review and use of static analysis tooling in our CI/CD pipeline.

6. BUSINESS CONTINUITY AND DISASTER RECOVERY

SecureVault operates across two AWS availability zones within each region to ensure
high availability. Daily automated backups are performed and stored in a separate
S3 bucket with versioning enabled. Backup integrity is tested on a quarterly basis.

Our recovery time objective (RTO) is 4 hours and our recovery point objective (RPO)
is 24 hours for full platform restoration. We conduct a tabletop business continuity
exercise annually.

7. SUPPLY CHAIN AND THIRD-PARTY RISK

We maintain a register of critical sub-processors and infrastructure providers.
Key providers include AWS (infrastructure), SendGrid (transactional email), and
Datadog (monitoring). Each provider is reviewed at contract renewal. We require
SOC 2 reports or equivalent certifications from Tier-1 suppliers.

We do not have a formal software composition analysis (SCA) process in place for
open-source dependencies, though we review dependency updates as part of our standard
development workflow.

8. CERTIFICATIONS AND AUDITS

SecureVault holds ISO 27001 certification (certificate number: ISOC-2023-44821,
valid through December 2025). We do not currently hold a SOC 2 report. Our ISO
27001 scope covers the design, development, operation, and support of our SaaS
document management platform.
